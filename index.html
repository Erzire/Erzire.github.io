About Me

I'm an Electrical Engineering student specializing in embedded engineering and PCB design for audio applications. As a lifelong musician, I'm passionate about creating innovative audio hardware that sits at the intersection of art and technology. My experience is highlighted by leading a team to build a complete hybrid synthesizer from concept to prototype, a project that involved system architecture, schematic and PCB layout in KiCad, and C++ firmware development. I'm aiming to contribute my skills in hardware design and embedded systems to the development of next-generation audio products.

A common thread through all my interests is a love for analog technologies and the freedom of manual control. Whether it's the improvisational structures of Jazz, the tactile nature of film photography, or the complete system control offered by Arch Linux, I'm drawn to understanding and tinkering with complex systems. This curiosity fuels my approach to engineering, blending methodical design with a creative drive to build something new and interesting.
